# Chapter 2: Intervention

She wandered the alien city for some time, before eventually finding those promised “terminals”. She rushed over and sat down. It looked like a forest-themed library computer from when she was a kid. She still wasn’t sure if everything being so *planty* was a stylistic choice or just how they built things. It didn’t really matter.

It lit up after seemingly detecting her presence, and the language changed to Terran. She was greeted by an oddly familiar interface, a search engine? She then noticed the title, “PlantPlantGo”. She would have pinched the bridge of her nose, if she still had one. The Affini were still as bad at naming things as they always have been.

She typed in her query. “Xenodrugs.” It pulled up a page of insultingly dumbed down and pushy “become a floret it's so cool\!” language. Like “ohh xenodrugs are how your owner makes you so happyyyy\! Smiles\!” She remembered the target audience was “*pets*”, so she tried to be more specific. “Xenodrug chemical composition”. The terminal showed, “for your safety, this information is only available to qualified xenoveterinarians. The benefits of visiting on-” she didn’t even finish reading before she tried to punch the screen out of anger. “The Overnet has more information MY ASS.” She noticed that the screen didn’t break, it just just softly stretched and went right back into place, unharmed. Was the fucking screen a plant too?

Before she could think more about either of those things, two Affini rushed into the room.

“We were alerted to some attempted damage in this area. Are you alright?”

Aurora, with complete confidence and smoothness (not at all, actually), said, “OH uh… yeah\! Everything is fine\! Say… uhh… do you all know where I could find the nearest…” she looked at the screen, “xenoveterinarian?”

The Affinis concern quickly turned to relieved happiness. “Oh, looking to be domesticated\! Well come with us we ca-”

“NOPE\! Nope\!” Aurora interrupted, “I can go there myself\! Please just tell me where it is\!”

The Affini looked a little confused, but pulled out a tablet and tapped something and a ship map with directions appeared on the terminal she was sitting at.

“If you need anything else, let us know. I promise you won’t regret it\!\~”

“Haha… yeahhh” Aurora halfheartedly tried to play along until they left. She looked at the map. At least she had a photographic memory now, since her eyes are literally cameras.

She made the long walk through the ship to the xenovet. She began to look at her surroundings. It looked so… peaceful? She saw the “pets” she had heard about, terrans on literal *leashes*, but… they looked happy? Unbothered, well cared for, content. Must be the drugs. She even briefly saw a *robot* pet. Huh, that Affini wasn’t kidding about them knowing about robots. And by the stars was that robot body a marvelously engineered one, it made her jealous. Wait, was she jealous of a *pet*? “Nothing comes for free,” she reminded herself.

She eventually arrived and walked in.

The Affini at the front desk greeted her.

“Ah, an independent looking to be domesticated\! Right this way\!”

Thankfully, she had time on her walk to plan her response.

“No, actually\! I’m here to see my friend, I know where her room is, I can go there myself.”

“How sweet\! Tell me the name and room number, and I’ll make sure you have approval from her owner.”

Shit. Yeah, of course. This wasn’t a doctor’s office, this was a *vet*, the patients here didn’t even have *rights*.

“Uh…. She doesn’t have an owner\! Yes\! She is an independent like me\! Happened to be injured nearby… and… *rescued by you wonderful Affini.*”

That last sentence came out a bit forced. The Affini’s expression shifted. Oh stars she noticed.

“Hmm, well we still need the name and room number to make sure you’re on the approved guest list.”

Shitshitshitshit.

“She is uhhh… unconscious\! She got here recently. She wouldn’t have been able to put me down.”

The Affini almost looked amused.

“How convenient. An independent, without an owner, who hasn’t been awake to approve you but somehow told you her room number, a room that you apparently know how to get to.”

Aurora stood there for a second.

“...yeeessss?”

The Affini laughed. “Oh petal, you know there’s no shame in seeking domestication, you don’t have to lie about it. Here, come with me, let’s get you all set up.”

The Affini got up and gestured towards a hallway full of rooms that looked oddly similar to the one she was in earlier.

“Nope\! Nope that’s uh… wellineedtoreallygetgoingnowsobye”

She quickly sprinted down the other open door, the one she hoped would lead her… honestly she didn’t even know what she was looking for. All she knew is that it wasn’t down the fucking domestication hallway.

She didn’t have any better plan than the last time she was caught in her lies, and she was quickly cornered by a few Affini again. Before any of them had time to act, she heard an all too familiar voice down the hall. Oh stars. It was him.

“Aurora\! There you are\! I heard about what happened and I rushed over as fast as I could.”

Ivory Lilium. The fucking *weed* who had been harassing her weekly since the Compact arrived. Ivory was, for an Affini, easy on the eyes. His body was vaguely humanoid, but still covered with the flowing designs the Affini are known for, not something resembling Terran clothing. His head was the most human-looking part, as if an Affini was trying to mimic the look of a young Terran man. He was not excessively masculine, his “hair” would be a few inches on a Terran, long enough to subtly curl. His face didn’t have anything resembling facial hair, nor was it obnoxiously chiseled like some kind of Greek god. Aurora hated that she found him hot. Being attracted to the Affini she was desperately trying to get rid of was NOT ideal.

Ivory continued, “But of all the places to be, you’re *here*\!”

The other Affini cornering her walked away and continued whatever they were doing. Guess they thought Ivory “had it handled”. Not like she could have fled from him alone anyways, so they were right.

“Oh little flower, I’m so happy you’re finally choosing domestication. You know, nobody ever regre-”

“NO I AM FUCKING *NOT*\!\!” Aurora snapped at him, “your fucking ‘friend’ *told* me that I could find *aaannnnyyyythhhinng* on the Overnet. I search for *one thing* and it says I have to come *here* to find out.”

Ivory looked confused, “what ‘information’ could you possibly need that is only found at a xenovet?”

Aurora looked annoyed, “oh, I don’t know, the one thing they do here, the thing you weirdos are known for, the thing I know you wish you could use on me\!”

Ivory was silent. She couldn’t tell if he didn’t know, or if he wanted her to say it.

“XENODRUGS\! I want information about XENODRUGS\!” She finally admitted.

Ivory asked, “what would you need those for?”

“I’ve improved my body, but my mind is still a simulation of my old one, in every annoying detail. As much as I hate it, you *freaks* are able to manipulate Terran psychology with immense precision. I may not be a psychologist, but as an engineer I’m not afraid to build on others’ work. Hopefully then I can fix my fuckin’ trauma and cure all my disorders and forget all the bullshit they di-”

Ivory suddenly looked horrified, his eye color changing. She quickly stopped talking and clasped her hands over her mouth. Well, there wasn’t a mouth there anymore, but the muscle memory of the gesture remained.

She had said too much. Over a year of hiding how deep the issues ran, hoping her constructed form would make that easier. He knew she had some issues, but she made sure to conceal their depth. She was trying to slowly earn her independence, to prove that what issues they could easily see weren’t enough to imprison her. And it was all undone because she couldn’t stop herself from running her mouth. Or speakers. Whatever.

Ivory looked determined, “I’m going to do what I should have done years ago.”

Ivory’s arms split into a dozen vines that picked her up quicker than she could react. She flinched, but knew that fighting back would just be a waste of her battery.

Ivory continued, “I’m going to get into that head of yours.”
