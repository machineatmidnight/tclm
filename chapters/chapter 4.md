# Chapter 4: Wardship

Aurora jolted awake, Ivory’s eyes having changed from blue to purple. Ah shit, he was finally doing something kind for her, and she just *had* to glitch right then, “stars, I’m so sorry, I think I must have a loose solder joint somewhere, I think I blacked out for a second. I haven’t been keeping up with maintenance as much as I should be.”

Hmm, she didn’t mean to let that last part out. She really needed to control her instinct to just ramble.

“Oh, it’s alright. We’ll make sure that all gets fixed.” Ivory responded, seemingly now less bothered by her poor maintenance.

Ivory turned around and went back to looking at the terminal. His earlier sense of urgency seemed gone. Odd.

Eventually he broke the silence, “so, I’m sure it won’t surprise you that this… ‘outburst’ of yours can’t go unaddressed.”

She hated how vague he was being.

He continued, “you will immediately be placed under my wardship.”

She remembered the other Affini mention that word. She asked, “what does that mean, like, specifically?”

“Essentially a temporary domestication. I will watch over you and care for you as if you were *my* pet. If you are able to recover, and I feel that you are fit to do so, your wardship will end, and you will be independent again.”

He turned to her with an annoyingly smug look on his face, “though, you could end the wardship early, if you’d rather truly become my pet.”

She rolled her eyes hard.

He got serious again, “Now, let’s go through some steps I’m sure you’ve seen before.”

He opened some weird small door in the wall and pulled out a few papers, a clipboard, and a pencil.

“Please fill out this mental health self-evaluation form. A bit more detailed than the ones you usually do, so make sure to look over each question carefully.”

She grabbed the forms and began to fill them out. It was different, and she was tired, so she spent more time on it than usual. She handed them back to him, and he started looking over them, a look of concern slowly showing on his face.

Still reading, he spoke, “goodness, it truly aches my core to see how much pain you kept inside all that time.”

“Huh? What’s so different??” She genuinely was confused.

A bit of smugness showing, he turned the papers to face her and said, “you were finally being honest this time.”

She looked at the papers briefly, and noticed… oh,, oh no,,, oh no no no. These weren’t her usual canned answers. She looked at the words she apparently wrote more closely. Each sentence was another issue she intentionally kept hidden, laid bare on the page.

She started panicking and looked up at him angrily, “you… you did something to me\! How did you get all of that out of me\!? I didn’t even *notice*\!”

“Are they not the truth?”

“Well yes of course they are, but I normally just don’t mention any of that. Wait, fuck, why did I say that? You’re making me say things\! How did you get in my mind\!? You’re forcing me to say these truths\! I mean truths\! I mean- FUCK\!\!”

Ivory’s smugness faded as he noticed her panic continue to increase. He tried to calm her, “petal, it’s ok, I promise, you don’t have to hi-”

“OF COURSE I HAVE TO HIDE IT\! Or else you *freaks* will fucking *own* me\! I worked this many years for my ind-”

Ivory let his concern quickly drop to focus on her. He interrupted her with one simple command.

“***DROP.***”

The surprise on her face very quickly faded back to that empty, thoughtless, floaty look she had earlier. She stared into the lovely patterns in Ivory’s eyes.

He let her stay there, floating, calm, for a little, before pulling back, letting her take a break.

“***TRUTH.***”

He ended the hypnotic command. The trigger got the use he wanted for now, and it was distressing her. He could use it later.

“***WAKE.***”

—

Another strange jolt. At least it seemed to calm her. She was glad that he was going to fix whatever solder joint that was, at least.

Ivory looked at her with a comforting and soft look in his eyes, “I know that, under the Accord, you might have had good reason to hide these, but things are different now. I know you’re scared, and I understand why, but I promise that the Compact only wants to help you. I’m not using these to lock you in some cell, but to make sure I’m caring for every need of yours.”

Tears began to show on her screen. She asked, softly, shaking, “how do I know?”

He smiled a bit more, “let me show you.”

He picked her up gently, softly wrapping vines around her, lifting her like the most fragile thing in the world. She was far too emotional to fight back. She wasn’t *hating* the touch right now.

He began to walk out of the room and down the hall, gently. Perhaps it would have been difficult for her to keep up on foot, but he did occasionally get passed by an Affini rushing past.

She didn’t really notice anything strange until they were outside the vet entirely.

She asked softly, “wait, where are we going?”

“I can take much better care of you with the tools in my hab, and I think you’ve had enough medical offices for one day.”

“Hab?”, her lack of experience being around Affini kept showing.

“A more general word for a house, apartment, dwelling, that kind of thing.”

She contemplated for a second before a thought shot through her mind. “Wait, you’re taking me home?”

Ivory giggled lightly, “I guess you could put it that way. You’re my *ward*, not just a patient. Would you really rather be back at the vet?”

She looked to the side and mumbled, “no…”

Ivory had to actively hold back the urge to hug her with all his might at hearing her intentionally simulated mumbled voice. She was just *so* cute.

It wasn’t too long before they arrived. Aurora did briefly wonder why he still wasn’t setting her down as he walked around, but she wasn’t going to *tell* him to.
