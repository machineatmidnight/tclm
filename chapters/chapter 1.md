# Chapter 1: “Stealth” mission

CRASH\!

The Terran ship that Aurora was aboard was hit by the first Affini vine. Like they’d done so many times, the vines bound and bored into the ship. Panic, racing steps, gunfire, eerie silence as they dropped one by one, at least that’s what Aurora heard from her hiding spot in the ship’s cargo bay. The odd smell that was rumored to be part of Affini attacks also finally made its way to her, but this also didn’t concern her. How could it? Her robot body merely sensed the faint smells in the air; it had no way to let them into her simulated consciousness.

Aurora is a robot, a machine. Her body resembles an average chubby Terran woman in general shape, size, and proportions. Her body is covered in large interconnected white and black triangular panels, with glowing magenta highlights. Where a Terran’s eyes and nose would be, she had a flat black screen, showing simple and stylized eyes to convey her emotion. The eyes were only solid magenta, appearing to have thick eyeliner, subtle eyelashes, and diamond-shaped pupils. Below her eyes was what appeared to be a mask, mostly black with magenta highlights, and a screen in the middle. The small screen on her mask showed a waveform of her speech, but nothing like a mouth. All of her emotions were conveyed through her expressive eyes. The most Terran thing about her was her hair, long, dark brown, and curly, no different than some Terran women, though obviously completely artificial.

Much quicker than she expected, all of the sounds of fighting back finished fading to nothing, and the ship creaked a little as the Affini vessel started pulling the captured ship back to… well… wherever they took captured ships. She wasn’t quite sure, but any large Affini ship should be good enough.

Soon enough, the ship shook hard and then came to a stop. Must’ve docked. She heard the Affini talking and shuffling their vines, moving about the ship, clearing it out, and soon enough, the sounds stopped entirely.

Aurora finally peeked out of her hiding spot, walking through the ship as silently as her heavy metal body would let her, seeing no sign of life. She finally saw the open main door and the inside of the Affini ship. Thankfully, the Affini seemed to have left, for now.

She slipped out and began looking around. The soft ground prevented her from making much noise, but her rigid metal body stood out like a sore thumb in the colorful organic Affini ship. She’d never been in one of these before, and hopefully won’t have to again.

Perhaps a little overconfident and used to being alone, it wasn’t long before she crossed paths with an Affini minding their own business. Seemingly consumed with whatever it was reading on its tablet, heading off to do whatever Affini do. Maybe if Aurora weren’t the least organic thing in this entire area of the ship, she wouldn’t have been spotted.

The Affini and Aurora both looked at each other. The Affini calmly stopped walking to look at what it had just stumbled on, while Aurora froze in sheer panic. *Right\! I had a plan for this\!*

She put on her best neutral expression, robotic body movements, and stood still.

“Hmm, I guess we missed one.” The Affini kneeled and asked, “What’s your name, little one?”

Aurora, putting on her most robotic voice, answered flatly, “GENERAL PURPOSE ASSISTANT UNIT. SERIAL NUMBER M3DY. REGISTERED TO TERRAN ACCORD.”

The Affini stood up and raised an eyebrow. He didn’t buy it. Shit.

“General purpose assistant unit?” the Affini asked, amused, “A remarkably complex and humanoid drone, aboard a vessel that tiny, not resembling any standard Terran technology, nor bearing any clear marks of its supposed Terran origin.”

Shitshitshitshitshit-

She tried to play up the act, “COMMAND NOT RECOGNIZED.”

He looked at her with an incredulous expression, as if he were almost laughing.

“And you seem to run such an oddly primitive program.” He kneeled, and got close, lowering his voice. “A ‘primitive’ assistant that had the cutest look of sheer panic behind those digital eyes of yours when you first saw me.”

Maybe letting her subconscious control the emotions in her “eyes” on her face-screen wasn’t the best idea. Wait shI-

She bolted off as fast as she could. Her motors were strong, and she could run much faster than a human, but she was also less than half the height of an Affini and could only move as fast as her humanoid legs could carry her.

Right after she made her first turn down the annoyingly large hallway, the Affini had caught up to her, and a few other Affini a little further down the hallway blocked her path. She was surrounded; the Affinis’ vines had spread out to truly block her in.

“Please, stop. Let us talk this out. We aren’t here to hurt you; we just want to ensure you’re cared for.” The Affini had a bit of concern in his voice, not for him, or the other Affini, but for her.

“I can care for myself just fine\!” Aurora yelled back, before lunging right into one of the other Affinis’ vines and trying to push them out of the way. How strong could an *organic* be anyway?

The vines all too eagerly moved out of the way before grabbing her tight as she was halfway through them, lifting her off the ground. Yeah, she should have seen that coming. She thrashed as violently as she could, but she didn’t get free. The vines moved with her limbs, but didn’t let her gain any leverage or escape. The Affini handed her over to the Affini nonchalantly and walked away, returning to whatever it was doing.

“It’s always cute seeing sophonts trying and failing to overpower us. The fight is over, petal. Let’s take you back with the others and get you all cleaned up.”

She knew the Affini were powerful, their technology being dozens of millennia ahead of anything else, but she had no idea that even one lone unarmed Affini was *this* strong.

The Affini started carrying her down the hallway. This was bad. Very bad. She fought her whole life for independence and control, and wasn’t about to let it get taken away after a few minutes of a very one-sided fight.

"O-o-o-ok, look, I lied, I have nothing to do with the Terrans, I do not work for them, I’m not allied with them, I don’t even really like them\! You don’t need to capture me\! And your whatever-drugs won’t work on me\!" She was a little less confident with that last sentence. She couldn’t just get *injected* the same way an organic could, but her brain was a near-perfect simulation of her old one, down to the chemical responses. The Affini were masters of technology. Could they find some way *in*? She hoped they never would. It would be best to not think about it and to keep up her façade of confidence. Maybe she was right, and maybe they wouldn’t try.

“We’ve helped countless species and have an intimate knowledge of digitized consciousness. I’m sure we’ll figure something out, or perhaps you’ll let us in.”

The Affini looked down at her with an annoyingly confident look. *Let them in?* Why would he think that after the trouble he went through to capture her in the first place?

“Like hell I would\! Put me the fuck down\! I don’t need your ‘help’\!”

The Affini turned into a smaller private room, closing the door behind him, and setting her down. Perhaps she should have been more specific. She backed up, staying in a tense, defensive pose.

The Affini sat down and calmly asked, “Now, tell me. What brings you here?”

He reached out and tried to grab her hand. She instantly pulled her hand back, her panicked look intensifying. She hoped he couldn’t make out the subtle hint of flustering in her emotions.

Her eyes darted around the room. She was trapped, moreso than ever before. The only way out was through these terrifying plant monsters.

She pleaded with her voice more full of fear than ever before, "Look, you don’t need to do this. I don’t want to harm any Affini. I just wanted to get some information, and then I would leave you all alone. I’m not some fucking mindless Terran soldier."

The Affini let out a small sigh, “I think we’ve already proven you aren’t capable of harming us anyway. That’s not why I took you here. I don’t think I need to explain why sneaking aboard an Affini ship is… concerning behavior.”

She rolled her eyes and mockingly said, “What happened to me ‘not being capable of harming you’?”

“You still might harm yourself.”

She was visibly shocked. Her extremely limited interactions with the Affini showed them as *predators*. Imperialists who wiped out a millenia old empire in a matter of years. Invaders who are hell-bent on turning their conquered foes into *pets*. And now their main concern was… her hurting herself?? But then, a memory popped in her mind. Her expression was one of mild anger and deep painful grief.

She spoke much softer than before, “Don't… pretend to care about me.”

The comment visibly concerned the Affini, his eyes shifting in color. He spoke with a new softness, “Oh, petal… of course we care about you. Why would we lie about that?”

Aurora’s tone slowly got angrier, “A better question, why *else* would you care? I’ve been hurt by monsters who hide behind a facade of ‘care’ before. I’m not going to let it happen again.”

The Affini’s concern grew and his eye color darkened, but he then rather abruptly chose to shift his focus, his tone becoming clinical, “Whether you like it or not, standard procedure says that, at this point, we have to do a full physical and mental evaluation so we can decide what’s best for you,” a bit of emotion returned to his voice as he lowered himself and got closer, “and we’ll make sure you get the care you need.”

Aurora was full of emotion, but she had no idea what to even think. Not like that could get her out of this. Just do the same as she always had, play it calm and they’ll leave her alone.

The Affini stood up and continued, “Now, please stand right over there so we can do the first physical scan.”

She hadn’t really looked at the room she was in. It looked like the doctors offices she used to go to… if they were made by crazy plant things. That was one thing she didn’t miss about being… biological.

She begrudgingly walked over to the corner of the room the Affini pointed to, and stood there. Was she supposed to do something?

The Affini input something into a tablet, mumbled something to himself in what she could only assume was the Affini language, and the half-plant-half-machine whirred to life, vine-looking tendrils tipped with strange technology came out, and began some kind of scan.

After one seemed to look her in the eye, a faint noise came from the Affini’s tablet.

“Ah, you already have records under the BXWC. Let’s have a look\!”

She quickly realized he must have meant that fucking Affini who’s been “checking on her” since the Compact took over. Is that who he worked for?

He began reading, “self-sustained but very isolated living, history of some mild mental health issues,” his face lit up, “oh wow\! *You* built this body? You were born Terran, the body was nearly complete by our first visit, and…” he stopped speaking, reading *something* very closely, then breaking the silence with “interesting…”

Awkward silence.

The Affini broke it, “well normally these scans are done while unconscious, but we need the scans to figure out *how* to do that, and we can use the time to talk.” That last part was said with a hint of smugness that irritated Aurora.

She spoke before he could, “I don’t want to fucking *talk*, I just want to get the information I need and then I can leave and you *xenos* won’t have to deal with me ever again.”

The Affini was intrigued, “Information… interesting. Well the Overnet has more of that than you’ve ever seen, and we have public terminals on this ship to use it. A look around the ship might be good for you.”

As annoyed as she was at his Affini arrogance, he had just told her exactly what she needed.

The Affini’s tablet made another sound, and the strange machine retracted into its resting state.

“Ah\!” the Affini went over to the tablet to read whatever report it had made. “Your body is… in working condition. Not enough damage to warrant wardship or domestication, but certainly enough that *I’m* concerned.” She rolled her eyes as he continued talking, “For now, the best we can do for a mental evaluation is a series of questions I’ll have you answer.”

He handed her a tablet with that series of all-too-familiar mental health questions that she could “pass” in her sleep. If by “pass”, you mean “lie that you’re completely healthy”. She handed it back and he read it.

“Hmm”, he seemed skeptical, but he moved forward. “While not quite normal, nothing today warrants immediate wardship or domestication. Your case manager will be notified, and I’d bet they’ll move up your next wellness check. But, for now, you are free to go.”

Wait, “notify her case manager”? Oh stars. If he finds out, god knows what he’s gonna make her do. She was on the clock to get her info.

She didn’t respond, she just silently followed him as he led her out to the main area of the ship.
