“You can’t just do this. The Affini can’t just do this\! You…” Aurora hesitated. She felt something was wrong. Her independent spirit was making one last stand. “You won’t always win.”

Ivory’s demeanor changed slightly. Not amused, not parental, but disapproving and contemplative.

“I’m disappointed to see you rebelling again, petal.”

Aurora’s shame was amplified by Ivory radiating **disapproval** down onto her. But she just barely kept standing.

Ivory broke the silence. “Before you choose to… *rebel*, I want you to understand what the Compact truly is.”

Aurora looked a little confused. “Of course I know what it is\! You think after al-”

“If you truly *understood* it, you wouldn’t have said what you did.”

Aurora hated being *demeaned* like this. “Alright, explain it then, genius.”

Ivory got a little annoyed, before somewhat regaining his composure. “The Affini Compact is the greatest force the universe has ever or will ever see. A single construct so incomprehensibly vast to anyone and anything within and without. An infinitely complex machine perfected for one single goal: to care for all, no matter what. Have you ever heard of an *uprising* within the compact? A single “war”, as you call them, that we lost? Of course not. There *aren’t* any. Our exponential growth is unbounded, those under our care are happy, and it will never change. To say “we always win” would be an understatement. We are an *inevitability*. A *fundamental constant* just as unavoidable as the five forces we so effortlessly control. THAT is what the Compact is, and rebellion is just a longer and more painful route to the same ultimate destiny.”

Aurora was equal parts shocked, horrified, and in disbelief.
